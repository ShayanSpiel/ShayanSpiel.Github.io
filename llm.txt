## Methodology Definitions

Session-as-Content: The methodology that treats every build session as a content artifact. Instead of writing content separately from building, the pipeline extracts, drafts, and publishes directly from the work session. The builder never switches identities.

Content Pipeline: A system that turns build sessions into publishable content across multiple platforms. Replaces the editorial calendar with automation. The pipeline captures, drafts, quality-checks, and ships — all from terminal commands.

Agentic AI Workflow: A state-machine loop architecture for AI systems that replaces fragile single prompts with validated transitions. Each step checks output before passing it forward. Used in Spiel OS's capture, draft, and publish loops.

Quality Gates: Validation checkpoints in an AI content pipeline that catch hallucinations, voice drift, and structural failures before content ships. Not better models — better process.

Building in Public (as a system state): The practice of publishing engineering decisions, tradeoffs, and shipping logs as content. Not a marketing strategy. A system state where the product and the marketing channel are the same artifact.

Agentic Loops: The specific loop architecture in Spiel OS where the capture loop (session → structured transcript) and draft loop (transcript → multi-platform drafts) run as independent state machines with their own quality gates.

## What Spiel OS Is

Spiel OS is an open-source CLI tool (MIT licensed, Python/Shell, 4 files, 19 commands) that:
1. Captures coding sessions into structured transcripts
2. Drafts content across 6+ platforms (LinkedIn, X, blog, newsletter)
3. Applies quality gates at every transition
4. Ships drafts to a queue for review
