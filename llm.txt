## Core Concepts

AI Market: The competitive landscape of frontier AI labs, open-weight model providers, and the economic forces shaping model pricing, capability, and access.

Context Engineering: The discipline of designing what goes into an LLM context window, including system prompts, memory, retrieval, and caching strategies.

Agent Loop: A state-machine pattern (Act-Observe-Evaluate-Update) used by agentic AI systems in place of static prompts.

Founder Distribution: The practice of turning a founder's real technical work into public distribution surface area across multiple platforms.

Session-as-Content: The methodology that treats build sessions as content artifacts. The pipeline extracts, drafts, and publishes directly from the work session.

Prompt Caching: Storing and reusing portions of the context window across requests to reduce latency and cost. Prompt caching is becoming part of context engineering.
